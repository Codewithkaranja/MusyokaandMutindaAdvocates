Your Rights During Arrest and Interrogation in Kenya

When dealing with law enforcement, knowledge is protection. Being respectful is important, but so is keeping your rights intact. If you are unsure, pause and consult your advocate.

Practical steps to protect yourself

  • Stay calm: avoid arguments or resistance
  • Ask the reason: request clarity on why you are being arrested
  • Request legal representation: speak to an advocate before recording statements
  • Do not sign what you don’t understand: insist on explanation and review
  • Capture key details: station name, officer details, and OB reference if possible

Bail and Bond in Machakos: What Courts Consider

Courts generally consider whether an accused person will attend court, interfere with witnesses, or pose a risk to the process. A strong bail application presents stability and cooperation clearly.

What strengthens a bail application

  • Fixed residence: clear address and community ties
  • Reliable sureties: credible people who can guarantee attendance
  • No flight risk: stable work, family ties, and cooperation
  • Non-interference: willingness to comply with conditions

Charge Sheets and Plea: Don’t Rush

Many people plead without fully understanding the charge or the long-term consequences. Before plea, ensure you understand: the elements of the offense, possible penalties, and whether there are legal grounds to challenge the charge.

What we review before plea

  • Whether the charge is properly framed and supported by evidence
  • Whether your rights were observed during arrest and investigations
  • Whether there are defenses available on facts or law
  • Whether a negotiated resolution is possible depending on circumstances

Trial Strategy: Evidence Wins Cases

A strong defense is built on evidence analysis, witness consistency, and procedural compliance. During trial, we focus on: challenging contradictions, testing the reliability of witnesses, and questioning the quality and legality of evidence.

Key defense approaches

  • Cross-examination: testing credibility and exposing inconsistencies
  • Evidence challenges: relevance, legality, and chain of custody concerns
  • Procedural issues: fairness, due process, and proper investigation steps
  • Defense case planning: documents, witnesses, and explanations that support your position

What to Bring When You Contact a Criminal Defense Lawyer

The more accurate your records, the better we can advise quickly.

  • Charge sheet or OB number (if available)
  • Any police documents or notices
  • Witness contacts (if relevant)
  • Any messages, photos, or documents tied to the incident
  • Timeline: where you were, when, and who was present

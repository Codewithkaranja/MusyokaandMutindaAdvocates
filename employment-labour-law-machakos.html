Employment Contracts: The Foundation of Workplace Peace

Many disputes start because terms were unclear. A strong employment contract reduces confusion and protects both parties.

Key clauses that reduce disputes

  • Job role & responsibilities: clear expectations and reporting lines
  • Compensation structure: salary, allowances, commissions, overtime where applicable
  • Leave and benefits: annual leave, sick leave and benefit entitlements
  • Disciplinary process: steps and documentation requirements
  • Confidentiality: protection of client data and business information
  • Termination & notice: lawful notice periods and exit obligations

Workplace Discipline: Process Matters

Discipline must be fair, documented, and consistent. For employers, a flawed process increases legal risk. For employees, knowing your rights helps you respond correctly and avoid costly mistakes.

What a fair disciplinary process usually includes

  • Clear allegations: what happened, when, and what rule/policy is involved
  • Time to respond: reasonable opportunity to prepare your explanation
  • Hearing: chance to be heard and present your position
  • Decision reasons: written reasons and outcome
  • Consistency: similar cases treated in similar ways
"In labour disputes, the strongest cases are built on records—letters, emails, minutes, payslips, and a clear timeline."
- Adv. Faith Musyoka

Unfair Termination: What Employees Should Know

Unfair termination claims often revolve around two questions: was there a valid reason, and was the process fair? If you believe your termination was unfair, preserve documents and seek advice quickly.

Documents that help in termination disputes

  • Employment contract and any addendums
  • Payslips and employment records
  • Warning letters / show-cause letters (if any)
  • Hearing invitations, minutes, and outcome letters
  • Emails/WhatsApp messages relevant to the dispute

Redundancy in Kenya: Getting the Process Right

Redundancy should follow lawful steps and fair criteria. Poor redundancy processes can create serious legal exposure. If redundancy is being planned or challenged, legal guidance helps protect the process and the parties.

Good redundancy planning focuses on

  • Clear business rationale and documentation
  • Fair selection criteria (non-discriminatory and consistent)
  • Proper notices and communication
  • Accurate dues calculation and settlement documentation

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a termination be challenged even if I signed a letter?

Sometimes, yes. Each case depends on facts, the process followed, and the documents involved. Seek advice early with your records.

Should I respond to a show-cause letter?

Yes—respond clearly and keep a copy. A well-prepared response can protect your position. If unsure, consult an advocate.

Can employment disputes be settled without court?

Many matters are resolved through negotiation and settlement agreements. Structured settlements save time and reduce costs.
